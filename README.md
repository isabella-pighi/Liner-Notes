# Liner Notes

Working notes and write-ups at the intersection of data science, AI, and the
messy problems they get pointed at. Each piece is literature-grounded and meant
to be read on its own.

## Music data: from deluge to strategy

A two-part series expanding an SXSW 2024 talk, updated to mid-2026, on making
sense of music numbers in the age of platforms and AI.

- **Part 1 — The Data Deluge** &nbsp;[[Markdown](data_deluge_paper.md) &middot; [PDF](Part1_Data_Deluge.pdf)]
  Why the industry has more data than ever and less certainty: datafication,
  where the numbers come from, why fans do not fit in a funnel, and the four
  families of trouble that make music analytics unreliable.
  References: [bibliography_part1.md](bibliography_part1.md).
- **Part 2 — Data Strategy** &nbsp;[[Markdown](data_strategy_paper.md) &middot; [PDF](Part2_Data_Strategy.pdf)]
  The practical playbook: strategy as a stack rather than a dashboard, the
  identifier-and-metadata backbone, causal inference and incrementality, the
  fan graph you own, privacy-preserving data sharing, and governing what AI
  learns from.
  References: [bibliography_part2.md](bibliography_part2.md).

## Agentic development for data science

- **Prove it, don't just install it** &nbsp;[[Markdown](METHODOLOGY.md) &middot; [PDF](Prove_it_dont_just_install_it.pdf)]
  A field guide to evidence-first development with an AI agent crew, drawn from
  the Bots Without Labels project: how a small team builds an unsupervised bot
  detector and why nothing ships until the claim behind it has survived a
  measurement. The detector itself, with full source and benchmarks, lives at
  [bots-without-labels](https://github.com/isabella-pighi/bots-without-labels).
