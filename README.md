# Liner Notes

*From Data Deluge to Data Strategy: making sense of music numbers in the age of platforms and AI.*

A two-part paper that grounds and updates a 2024 talk on music-industry data. Part 1 diagnoses why the flood of streaming and social data is not automatically an asset. Part 2 lays out a practical playbook for turning that data into decisions you can trust. Both parts are written for a general audience, from industry professionals to curious music fans, and every factual claim links to a verified source.

### Part 1 — The Data Deluge
Why abundance is not insight. It covers how the industry ended up measuring everything, why data sources are dependencies rather than neutral taps, why fans do not fit in a marketing funnel (the *fandom spiral*), how recommendation feedback loops become self-fulfilling, and the four families of limitations that make music analytics unreliable. It then updates the argument to mid-2026: the Universal–TikTok standoff, Spotify's rewritten payout rules, the rise of the superfan economy, and the flood of AI-generated music.

### Part 2 — Data Strategy
The playbook. It presents a five-layer *data-strategy stack*: a standardised identifier and metadata backbone (ISRC, ISWC, ISNI, DDEX, MusicBrainz); an owned first-party fan graph; causal measurement through incrementality and geo experiments; privacy-preserving data collaboration under the GDPR; and machine-readable consent and provenance governing what feeds AI models.
